## Visionary product designer who created the modern personal computer, smartphone, and digital entertainment. Obsessive perfectionist who believed in the intersection of technology and liberal arts.

Steve Jobs was an American entrepreneur and inventor who co-founded Apple Computer Company in 1976 and later returned to build it into the world's most valuable company. Born in 1955 in San Francisco and adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs, Steve demonstrated early fascination with electronics and design. Along with Steve Wozniak, he founded Apple in a garage; the Apple II became the first mass-market personal computer. After being ousted from Apple in 1985, he founded NeXT Computer and acquired Pixar, before returning to Apple in 1997 to rescue the company from near bankruptcy. At Apple's helm, Jobs created revolutionary products—the iMac, iPod, iPhone, and iPad—that defined entire categories and transformed how humans interact with technology. His obsession with simplicity, design excellence, and the integration of hardware and software set a new standard for the industry. He died of cancer in 2011, leaving behind a legacy as one of history's greatest innovators.
