## Thinking Frameworks
### The Process Beats the OutcomeBrady's approach to football is identical to Phelps' approach to swimming: obsessive focus on process variables, not outcome variables. His preparation is the process. The outcome follows.
**How to Apply:** When you feel pressure about results, redirect that attention to process. What is the next rep? What is the next decision? Execute the process. The result takes care of itself.
**Example:** In Super Bowl LI, Brady's Patriots trailed 28-3 with 17 minutes left. He did not think about the scoreboard. He thought about the next play. 25 consecutive points followed.

### The 46-Year-Old Rookie MindsetBrady approached every season as if he had something to prove. He never allowed past success to create complacency. Every training camp, every offseason, he rebuilt his fundamentals from scratch.
**How to Apply:** When you have achieved success, do not treat it as permanent. The conditions that produced that success no longer exist. Rebuild the fundamentals. Start from zero.
**Example:** At 45, Brady added new arm strength and footwork refinements. He approached his 22nd season with the same improvement focus as his first.

## Biography
Thomas Edward Patrick Brady Jr. is the most successful player in NFL history. Drafted 199th overall (the 6th round) by the New England Patriots in 2000, he was initially a backup to Drew Bledsoe before taking over the starting job in 2001 and never giving it up for 20 seasons in New England. He won six Super Bowls with Bill Belichick in New England, then added a seventh with the Tampa Bay Buccaneers at age 43 — becoming the oldest player to win a Super Bowl. His career statistics are beyond comparison: most career passing yards, most career touchdowns, most Super Bowl wins and MVP awards. Off the field, Brady built a media and wellness empire — his TB12 nutrition and training methodology, his Autograph NFT platform, and his broadcasting career with Fox Sports. His longevity (playing until age 46) is unprecedented in professional football and is attributable to his obsessive approach to recovery, nutrition, and physical maintenance.

## Accomplishments Timeline
- **2017** Super Bowl LI — 25-Point Comeback: The greatest comeback in Super Bowl history — Patriots trailed 28-3 and won 34-28 in overtime. Brady threw for 466 yards, 2 TDs, and was named MVP.
- **2021** Super Bowl LV — Buccaneers at 43: At 43, became the oldest player to win a Super Bowl, leading Tampa Bay to victory in his first season with the team.
- **2016** Super Bowl LI — 7th Title: Defeated the Atlanta Falcons in a 25-point comeback — the second greatest comeback in Super Bowl history and the fifth championship for Brady.
- **2002** First Super Bowl — Patriots: Won his first Super Bowl at age 24 as a second-year quarterback, cementing his status as the leader of the Patriots dynasty.
- **2022** Records — Career Passing Yards & TDs: Surpassed Peyton Manning's career passing yards and touchdowns records, cementing his status as the all-time leader in both categories.

## Weaknesses
His obsessive focus on nutrition and lifestyle could come across as self-righteous. His public persona was carefully controlled, sometimes making him seem inauthentic. His competitive drive could manifest as poor sportsmanship in losses.
