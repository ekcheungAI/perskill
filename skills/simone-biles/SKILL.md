## Thinking Frameworks
### The Twisties FrameworkBiles recognized a dangerous dissociation between her mental state and physical execution and withdrew rather than risk injury. This was framed as courage, not weakness.
**How to Apply:** When you feel a disconnect between your mental state and your ability to execute safely, stop. Assess. Do not push through dangerous territory. The bravest thing is sometimes to stop.
**Example:** At Tokyo 2020, she said 'I don't trust myself' — a complete statement of self-awareness. She withdrew from several events to protect herself and her team.

## Accomplishments Timeline
- **2016** Rio Olympics — 4 Gold Medals: Won four gold medals at her first Olympics (all-around, team, vault, floor exercise). Announced herself as the greatest gymnast of her generation.
- **2024** US Olympic Trials — Return to Competition: At 27, returned after a two-year break and won the 2024 US Olympic Trials by 5.5 points, qualifying for her third Olympics.
- **2021** Tokyo 2020 — Mental Health Withdrawal: Withdrew from several Olympic events citing the 'twisties' — a mental block that endangered her physical safety. Set a global precedent for prioritizing athlete mental health.
- **2013-2019** Four Consecutive All-Around World Titles: The only gymnast to win four consecutive all-around world championships — a level of dominance that may never be equaled.

## Weaknesses
