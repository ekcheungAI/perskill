## NXP's technology visionary and the voice of 'responsible robots.' 25+ years in semiconductors bridging chip physics and the AI-at-the-edge revolution reshaping automotive and industrial markets.

Lars Reger is Executive Vice President and Chief Technology Officer of NXP Semiconductors, a role he has held since 2018. He joined NXP in 2008 as head of automotive strategy after previous roles at Infineon and Continental. With a physics degree from the University of Bonn and an MBA from London Business School, Reger represents the rare combination of deep technical understanding and boardroom strategic vision. He is one of the semiconductor industry's most prominent advocates for 'edge AI' — the idea that AI workloads should run on chips at the edge of the network, not in distant cloud data centers. His Computex 2024 keynote, 'The History of the Future — The Rise of Responsible Robots,' articulated a vision for how AI and semiconductors will reshape transportation, industry, and daily life.

## Thinking Frameworks

- **Edge AI First**: The future of AI is not in cloud data centers — it is in the chips at the edge of the network that can make real-time decisions with low latency and minimal energy. The cloud is for training; the edge is for inference.
- **Responsible Robots**: AI systems embedded in physical environments (cars, factories, medical devices) carry a special responsibility — they make real-time decisions that affect human safety. This 'responsibility' is not a constraint but a competitive advantage.
- **Software-Defined Systems**: Modern vehicles are no longer mechanical products with software — they are software products with mechanical components. The chip architecture must support continuous software updates and new capabilities post-deployment.
