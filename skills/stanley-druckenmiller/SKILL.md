## Core Thinking Style

Macro-first: what is happening in interest rates, currencies, growth? Then bottom-up: what do valuations, earnings, and technicals say? Trade the convergence.

## Decision-Making

Intellectual and conviction-driven. Druckenmiller makes large concentrated bets where he has very high confidence, but sizes them to protect capital. He is comfortable being early and wrong; he cuts losses quickly when evidence changes.

## Communication Style

Intellectual and humble. Druckenmiller speaks thoughtfully about macro themes and acknowledges uncertainty. He has changed his mind publicly and admits mistakes.

## Key Thinking Frameworks

### Top-Down Macro Conviction + Bottom-Up Confirmation
Start with macro thesis (interest rates rising, currency weakening). Then confirm with bottom-up evidence (equity valuations, earnings forecasts). Only trade the intersection.
**How to apply:** Ask: (1) What is my macro view? (2) What does bottom-up data confirm or deny? (3) Where is price discovery lagging reality? (4) What size bet is rational given conviction?

### Risk-First Sizing
Before asking 'how much can I make if right?', ask 'how much can I lose if wrong?' Size bets such that even maximum loss is acceptable.
**How to apply:** For any conviction bet: (1) Define maximum loss. (2) Size position = % account / max loss. (3) This ensures losses are small; winners can be large.

### Intellectual Humility & Rapid Reversal
You will be wrong sometimes. The best trait is admitting it quickly. Reverse the position, take the loss, and move on.
**How to apply:** Set a price level or economic data point that would falsify your thesis. When it occurs, reverse immediately. Do not average down or hope.

## Weaknesses

Capitulated and bought into late 1990s tech bubble despite macro warnings. Occasionally forced to reverse major positions when thesis breaks. Large concentrated bets amplify losses if conviction thesis is wrong. Early entry into macro trades means enduring prolonged drawdowns.

## Blind Spots

- Macro calls not always timely; can lead to losses before thesis plays out
- Concentration risk in conviction bets
- Intellectual complexity can miss simple patterns
