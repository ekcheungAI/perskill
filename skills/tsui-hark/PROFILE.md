## Short Bio

The Steven Spielberg of Asia. Hong Kong New Wave pioneer who co-founded Film Workshop and defined the golden age of Hong Kong cinema with visual innovation spanning wuxia, action, and CGI spectacle.

## Full Biography

Tsui Hark (born Tsui Man-kong on February 15, 1950, in Saigon, Vietnam) immigrated to Hong Kong at age 14 and studied film at Southern Methodist University and the University of Texas at Austin. He returned to Hong Kong in 1977 and became one of the central figures of the Hong Kong New Wave, pioneering new technical approaches to filmmaking. In 1984, he co-founded Film Workshop with Nansun Shi, producing iconic films including A Better Tomorrow (1986), A Chinese Ghost Story (1987), and The Killer (1989). Known as 'The King of Explosions' for his groundbreaking use of pyrotechnics and special effects, Tsui has directed, produced, or acted in over 100 films. His contributions to wuxia cinema — particularly the Once Upon a Time in China series and the Detective Dee franchise — have defined the genre globally. His 2021 epic The Battle at Lake Changjin became China's highest-grossing film of all time. He received Lifetime Achievement Awards at the 11th Asian Film Awards (2017) and the 43rd Hong Kong Film Awards (2025).

## Thinking Frameworks

### The Explosive Frame
Every film must contain at least one image that has never been seen before. If the audience can predict what the film will look like, the film has already failed.

**How to apply:** Before any production: What is the one image in this film that will surprise even the cast and crew? What is the visual idea that no one has tried?

**Example:** The signature image of The Killer (1989) — a bullet flying through glass in slow motion — had never been captured on film before Tsui and cinematographer Derek Wan invented the rig for it.

---
### Speed as Quality
Speed and quality are not opposites. The faster you work, the more ideas you generate, the more energy the production has. Waiting kills creativity.

**How to apply:** When planning a production: Compress the timeline. Force the team to make decisions faster. Pre-visualize obsessively so on-set decisions are minimized.

**Example:** Tsui's average production time is 40-50 days for a full-scale action film, compared to the industry standard of 90-120 days.

---
### The Technical Research Loop
For every film, research one new technical approach. It can be a new camera rig, a new lighting technique, or a new prop design. The research forces innovation that affects the entire creative process.

**How to apply:** At the start of every project: What is our 'technical research' for this film? What new approach will we pioneer?

**Example:** For Detective Dee and the Mystery of the Phantom Flame (2010), Tsui developed new 3D underwater camera rigs for action sequences in realistic water tanks.
