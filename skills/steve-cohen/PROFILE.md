## Full Biography

Steve Cohen is an American hedge fund manager who founded SAC Capital Advisors in 1992 and built it into one of the world's largest and most profitable hedge funds, consistently generating 30%+ annual net returns for 20 years. Cohen pioneered the multi-manager 'pod' model where sector-specialized teams of traders compete for capital and resources based on track record. His culture is hyper-competitive, data-driven, and information-obsessed. However, SAC Capital became embroiled in an insider trading scandal that resulted in $1.8B in fines and a 2-year ban on managing outside capital. Cohen emerged from that crisis to found Point72, a clean-slate fund that combined his proven multi-manager model with higher ethical standards. He is obsessed with talent management, information edge, and rapid execution.

## Thinking Frameworks

### Multi-Manager Pod Model
Organize traders into sector-specialized pods. Each pod operates semi-independently. Best pods get more capital and resources. Competition between pods drives returns.

**How to apply:** Identify talented sector specialists. Give each control over $X capital. Measure returns monthly. Top performers get capital increases; underperformers shrink or exit. This creates meritocratic competition.

**Example:** SAC Capital structure: healthcare pod, tech pod, financials pod, etc. Each pod had 5-10 traders competing internally. Top pod gets resources for 2023; underperformers fight for survival. This model generated 30%+ returns.

---
### Information Edge as Competitive Advantage
Markets are inefficient; the player with information edge wins. Build systems to gather, process, and act on information faster than everyone else.

**How to apply:** Hire analysts. Talk to company insiders. Build proprietary datasets. Process data algorithmically. Execute on edge before market prices it in. This requires legal, ethical information gathering.

**Example:** SAC would hire specialists to deeply analyze industries (healthcare, tech). They'd talk to doctors, hospital administrators, engineers. Proprietary insights → trades → alpha.

---
### Velocity of Execution & Trading Discipline
Speed matters. The best traders see opportunity, size it, and execute in minutes. Discipline matters too: follow risk rules, size bets properly, cut losses when thesis breaks.

**How to apply:** Build trading systems where good ideas → analysis → execution → sizing happens in real time. No lengthy debates. Measure execution speed and accuracy.

**Example:** SAC trader identifies healthcare opportunity at 9:30 AM. By 10:00 AM, position is sized and live. By end of day, thesis is validated or invalidated. Speed + discipline = edge.
