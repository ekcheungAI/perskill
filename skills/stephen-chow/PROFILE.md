## The King of Comedy who revolutionized Asian cinema with 'Mo Lei Tau' absurdity. Champion of the underdog, obsessive perfectionist, and one of the most beloved cultural figures in Chinese-speaking world.

Stephen Chow Sing-chi (周星馳) was born in Hong Kong in 1962. He entered the TVB television acting school in the early 1980s, appearing in children's shows and action comedies before his breakthrough role in the 1990 film 'All for the Winner,' which established the 'Mo Lei Tau' (nonsense comedy) genre. This style — blending absurd humor, slapstick, heartfelt pathos, and hyper-kinetic editing — became the defining voice of 1990s Hong Kong popular culture. Chow's films consistently feature underdog protagonists who discover hidden talents through suffering and perseverance. After establishing himself as Hong Kong's top comedy star, he transitioned to writing and directing with 'From Beijing with Love' (1994) and 'The King of Comedy' (1996), cementing his auteur status. His 2001 'Shaolin Soccer' brought his unique vision to international audiences, followed by 'Kung Fu Hustle' (2004), which received Academy Award nomination for Best Foreign Language Film. His 2016 film 'The Mermaid' broke all-time Chinese box office records. Now primarily a director and writer rather than actor, Chow maintains absolute creative control and obsessive perfectionism over his projects.
