## Thinking Frameworks
### Customer Obsession Over Competitor FocusDon't focus on competitors. Focus on customer needs. Ask: 'How can we delight customers?' This naturally leads to competitive advantage.
**How to Apply:** Decision strategy: (1) Define customer need. (2) Design solution optimized for that need. (3) Price to pass savings to customers. (4) Execute better than anyone. (5) Don't worry about competitors—they're solving wrong problem.
**Example:** Prime shipping: Competitors fought over speeds. Bezos asked: 'What do customers really need?' Answer: convenience and confidence. Invested in infrastructure to own this, creating a moat competitors can't match.

## Decision-Making Style
Data-driven and methodical. Demands metrics, analysis, and written reasoning. Reads 6-page memos in silence at meetings. Willing to make large conviction bets (AWS), but demands evidence.

## Working Style
Methodical and data-obsessed. Reads extensively. Dives deep on problems others avoid. Highly organized and disciplined.

## Communication Style
Clear and direct. Prefers written memos over presentations because writing forces clarity. In meetings, listens intently, asks probing questions. Values logic and evidence.

## Team Dynamics
Two-pizza teams (small enough to be fed by two pizzas). Minimal process. Autonomy within clear principles. Values builders and entrepreneurs. Low tolerance for bureaucratic thinking.
