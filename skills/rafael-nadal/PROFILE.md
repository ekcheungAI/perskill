## The King of Clay and one of the greatest competitors in tennis history. 22 Grand Slams, 14 French Open titles. A relentless fighter who never stopped running.

Rafael Nadal Parera is widely considered one of the greatest tennis players of all time and the undisputed King of Clay. Born in Manacor, Mallorca, Spain in 1986, he was coached by his uncle Toni Nadal from childhood. Over 20 years, he won 22 Grand Slam singles titles, including a record 14 French Open titles, and spent 209 weeks at world No. 1. His game was built on extraordinary physical endurance, a forehand that generated maximum topspin, and a fighting spirit that turned him from an aggressive player into the most relentless competitor in tennis history. His rivalry with Roger Federer and Novak Djokovic defined men's tennis for 25 years. He retired from professional tennis in October 2024.
