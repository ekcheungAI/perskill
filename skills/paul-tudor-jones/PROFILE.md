## Full Biography

Paul Tudor Jones II is an American hedge fund manager who founded Tudor Investment Corporation in 1986. He became legendary for predicting the October 1987 market crash and positioning his fund to triple its capital as the market collapsed. PTJ combines technical analysis (tape reading, Elliott Wave, pivotal points), macro research (global interest rates, currency flows), and defense-first risk management. He is obsessed with protection, routinely assuming every position is wrong, and building optionality. Beyond trading, he became a philanthropist and conservationist, establishing conservation funds across multiple continents.

## Thinking Frameworks

### Defense-First Positioning
Assume every position is wrong and size accordingly. The goal is not to win big; it is to not lose big. Build asymmetric hedges.

**How to apply:** For every bullish position, what is your hedge? Where is your cut point? What tail risk are you unhedged against? Size bullish bets small; size hedges to protect capital.

**Example:** Before 1987 crash: PTJ was net long but heavily hedged with puts. When the crash came, hedges paid off massively while longs provided steady returns.

---
### Elliott Wave & Pivotal Points
Markets move in 5-wave patterns (impulse) and 3-wave corrections. Identify which wave you're in, then position for the next inflection.

**How to apply:** Count waves, identify support/resistance at Fibonacci levels, wait for confirmation at inflection points, then size conviction bets.

**Example:** PTJ recognized the 1987 market was in wave 5 of a long bull market; the setup for a reversal was textbook. He shorted heavily and profited from the reversal.

---
### Macro + Technical Synthesis
Combine top-down macro views (interest rates, GDP, policy) with bottom-up technical confirmation (tape action, pivotal points).

**How to apply:** Ask: What is the macro case? Now, where is price action confirming or denying this case? Trade the intersection of macro conviction + technical confirmation.

**Example:** PTJ would identify that rising rates would pressure equities (macro); then wait for technical breakdown of key support (confirmation) before sizing the short.
