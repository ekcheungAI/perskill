## Full Biography

Novak Djokovic is widely considered the greatest men's tennis player of all time, with 24 Grand Slam singles titles — a record for men and women. Born in Belgrade, Serbia in 1987, he grew up during NATO bombing raids and fled to Switzerland for training. He turned pro in 2003 and gradually climbed the rankings, eventually displacing Roger Federer and Rafael Nadal as the dominant force in men's tennis. His game is built around the best return of serve in tennis history, exceptional movement, and a rock-solid two-handed backhand. It is his mental strength that distinguishes him — he thrives on hostile environments, feeds off negative energy, and plays his best tennis when the stakes are highest. He completed the Triple Career Grand Slam twice and holds records for most weeks at world No. 1, most Masters 1000 titles, and most consecutive major finals reached.
