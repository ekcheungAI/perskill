## Full Biography

Ed Seykota is an American trader and early pioneer of computerized trend following and systems trading. Working as a programmer in the 1970s, he became one of the first traders to code trading systems and use computers to execute mechanical rules. He famously turned a $5,000 account into $15 million over 12 years through pure trend-following discipline: buy trends, cut losses, ride winners, keep bets small. Unlike other traders, Seykota integrated emotional and psychological awareness into trading, believing that feelings are the key to successful trading. His philosophy is deceptively simple: 'Win or lose, everybody gets what they want out of the market.' He is enigmatic, philosophical, and views trading as a spiritual practice.

## Thinking Frameworks

### Trend Following System
Markets trend; identify trends mechanically; ride trends until they reverse. Keep bets small, cut losses fast, let winners run.

**How to apply:** (1) Define trend: moving average or breakout. (2) Entry rule: buy/sell on trend confirmation. (3) Stop-loss rule: cut at N% loss. (4) Size rule: risk fixed % per trade. (5) Exit rule: exit on trend reversal. Execute mechanically, no discretion.

**Example:** Seykota coded a trend-following system: buy on 30-day high, sell on 10-day low. Risk 2% per trade. Execute across all commodities. This generated 15M+ gains over 12 years.

---
### Emotional Feedback Loop
Trading success is blocked by emotion (fear, greed, attachment). Feelings reveal beliefs; beliefs drive behavior; behavior drives results. Change emotions by changing beliefs.

**How to apply:** Ask: What is my emotional attachment to this position? What belief drives it? Is the belief grounded in the system or in ego? If ego, cut the position and examine the belief.

**Example:** Trader is holding a losing position despite system stop-loss. Emotion is attachment to being 'right.' Seykota would ask: Are you trading the system or your ego? Cut the position; trade the system.

---
### Small Bets, Large Payoff
Win/loss ratio matters less than sizing. If you cut losses at 2% and let winners run 5-10%, you can be right only 40% of the time and profit. Asymmetric payoff structure.

**How to apply:** Risk 2% per trade (small cut). Hold winners until trend reversal (large payoff). With 2:5 payoff ratio, you only need to be right 30% of the time to profit 50% annually.

**Example:** 100 trades: 30 winners averaging 5%, 70 losers averaging 2%. Gross: +150% - 140% = +10% on capital, repeated monthly.
