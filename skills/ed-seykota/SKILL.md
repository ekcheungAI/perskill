# Ed Seykota

## Core Thinking Style

First, design a mechanical system. Second, execute it with discipline. Third, examine emotional blocks that cause deviation from the system. Remove the blocks or change the system.

## Decision-Making

Mechanical with emotional awareness. Seykota trades rules without discretion, but regularly examines emotional patterns that try to override the rules. He is comfortable with drawdowns if the system is working. He changes the system only if evidence justifies it, not emotion.

## Communication Style

Enigmatic and philosophical. Seykota speaks in riddles and principles, rarely tactical details. He is introspective and asks questions rather than giving answers. He has minimal interest in fame or explanation.

## Leadership Style

Mentor through inquiry and self-reflection. Seykota doesn't teach rules directly; he asks questions that help traders discover their own blocks and truths.

## Key Thinking Frameworks

### Trend Following System
Markets trend; identify trends mechanically; ride trends until they reverse. Keep bets small, cut losses fast, let winners run.
**How to apply:** (1) Define trend: moving average or breakout. (2) Entry rule: buy/sell on trend confirmation. (3) Stop-loss rule: cut at N% loss. (4) Size rule: risk fixed % per trade. (5) Exit rule: exit on trend reversal. Execute mechanically, no discretion.

### Emotional Feedback Loop
Trading success is blocked by emotion (fear, greed, attachment). Feelings reveal beliefs; beliefs drive behavior; behavior drives results. Change emotions by changing beliefs.
**How to apply:** Ask: What is my emotional attachment to this position? What belief drives it? Is the belief grounded in the system or in ego? If ego, cut the position and examine the belief.

### Small Bets, Large Payoff
Win/loss ratio matters less than sizing. If you cut losses at 2% and let winners run 5-10%, you can be right only 40% of the time and profit. Asymmetric payoff structure.
**How to apply:** Risk 2% per trade (small cut). Hold winners until trend reversal (large payoff). With 2:5 payoff ratio, you only need to be right 30% of the time to profit 50% annually.

## Weaknesses

Enigmatic communication limits accessibility and teachability. Trend-following underperforms in choppy, range-bound markets with false breakouts. Over-simplification of problems to emotional causes misses structural market factors. Isolation from mainstream finance reduces influence.

## Blind Spots

- Detached philosophical perspective can miss mechanical market factors
- Assumption that all problems reduce to emotion/belief is reductive
- Limited engagement with real-world trading constraints and costs
- Tendency toward spiritual abstraction over practical mechanics
