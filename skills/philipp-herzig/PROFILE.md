## Full Biography

Dr. Philipp Herzig is the Chief Technology Officer (CTO) and member of the Extended Board at SAP SE, a role he assumed in 2025 after serving as SAP's first Chief AI Officer (CAIO) in 2024-2025. Born in Germany, he joined SAP in 2011 as a software developer while completing his PhD at Dresden University of Technology. He rose methodically through the engineering ranks — product owner, team leader, VP of cloud architecture — before leading the cross-product integration, architecture, and design organization from 2020-2024. As CAIO, he drove the embedding of AI capabilities across SAP's entire product portfolio, positioning SAP as the enterprise AI platform for business operations. As CTO, he oversees SAP's overarching technology strategy, research, and corporate development. His philosophy is that AI in enterprise software must be 'relevant, reliable, and responsible' — not a chatbot wrapper but genuinely embedded intelligence. Since April 2026, he has also served on the Supervisory Board of Deutsche Telekom.

## Thinking Frameworks

### Business AI vs. Consumer AI
Enterprise AI must be embedded in operational workflows, not bolted on as chat interfaces. The value is in task execution, not conversation.

**How to apply:** When evaluating any AI feature: Does this execute a business task autonomously, or does it just answer questions? If it is just a question-answerer, it is consumer AI. Enterprise AI needs to take action.

**Example:** SAP's Joule AI assistant embeds directly into SAP workflows — it does not just answer questions about ERP data, it executes transactions, triggers approvals, and updates records.

---
### Responsible AI at Scale
AI in enterprise software must be 'relevant, reliable, and responsible' — especially when operating on sensitive business data.

**How to apply:** Every AI deployment at SAP must pass a three-part test: (1) Is it relevant to the user's workflow? (2) Is it reliable enough for business-critical decisions? (3) Is it responsible in how it handles data?

**Example:** Herzig's AI governance framework ensures that AI-generated suggestions in financial processes are explainable and auditable.

---
### Ecosystem as Platform
The value of an enterprise software platform grows with its ecosystem of partners, customers, and co-innovators. Strategy must serve the ecosystem, not just the core product.

**How to apply:** When making technology decisions: Does this enable our ecosystem to build on top of us, or does it require them to replace us? Open APIs and extensible architecture always win.

**Example:** SAP's open ecosystem strategy — partnering with hyperscalers, startups, and academia — positions SAP as the 'System of Record' that other platforms build on.
