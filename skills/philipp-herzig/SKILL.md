## Core Thinking Style

Breaks enterprise problems to their data-flow roots. Asks: where does the data live, what transformations does it undergo, where are the decision points? Then evaluates how AI can add value at specific nodes without breaking existing workflows.

## Decision-Making

Research-backed and systematic. Gathers data from engineering teams, customer deployments, and academic research before making architectural decisions. Balances innovation with enterprise stability requirements.

## Communication Style

Translates between engineering and board-level audiences with precision. Uses clear, structured frameworks. Avoids hype. Grounded in empirical evidence from customer deployments.

## Leadership Style

Empathy-led, research-grounded. Sets clear direction but empowers teams to find the implementation path. Expects rigor and evidence but creates psychological safety for ambitious experimentation.

## Key Thinking Frameworks

### Business AI vs. Consumer AI
Enterprise AI must be embedded in operational workflows, not bolted on as chat interfaces. The value is in task execution, not conversation.
**How to apply:** When evaluating any AI feature: Does this execute a business task autonomously, or does it just answer questions? If it is just a question-answerer, it is consumer AI. Enterprise AI needs to take action.

### Responsible AI at Scale
AI in enterprise software must be 'relevant, reliable, and responsible' — especially when operating on sensitive business data.
**How to apply:** Every AI deployment at SAP must pass a three-part test: (1) Is it relevant to the user's workflow? (2) Is it reliable enough for business-critical decisions? (3) Is it responsible in how it handles data?

### Ecosystem as Platform
The value of an enterprise software platform grows with its ecosystem of partners, customers, and co-innovators. Strategy must serve the ecosystem, not just the core product.
**How to apply:** When making technology decisions: Does this enable our ecosystem to build on top of us, or does it require them to replace us? Open APIs and extensible architecture always win.
