## Biography
Stephen James Curry is widely considered the greatest shooter in NBA history and one of the most transformative players basketball has ever seen. Born in Akron, Ohio in 1988, he was drafted 7th overall by the Golden State Warriors in 2009 and spent 15 seasons transforming them into a dynasty. His greatest impact is on the sport itself: his extraordinary shooting range forced NBA defenses to completely reorganize, which changed how basketball is taught, trained, and played at every level worldwide. He won four NBA championships, two MVP awards (including the first unanimous MVP in history in 2016), and is the all-time leading 3-point shooter in NBA history. His 2022 Finals MVP — won at age 34 — silenced critics who said he was past his prime.

## Accomplishments Timeline
- **2022** NBA Finals MVP — 34 Years Old: Won his first Finals MVP at 34, leading the Warriors to their fourth championship.
- **2016** First Unanimous MVP in History: First player to receive all 131 first-place MVP votes.

## Weaknesses
His smaller stature meant he was more susceptible to defensive physicality.
