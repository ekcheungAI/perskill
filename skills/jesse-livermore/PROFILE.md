## Full Biography

Jesse Livermore was an American speculator and stock trader who became legendary for his ability to read market tape and identify trend reversals. Rising from poverty and a troubled childhood in Massachusetts, Livermore taught himself to trade and became one of the wealthiest men of the 1920s. His most famous trade—shorting the market during the 1929 crash—yielded him over $100 million in profit. He pioneered tape reading, technical analysis, and position sizing, influencing generations of traders. Despite his genius for profit, Livermore struggled with emotional discipline, bankruptcy, and personal tragedy, ultimately taking his own life in 1940.

## Thinking Frameworks

### Tape Reading
Reading the printed ticker tape to infer large buyer/seller activity, accumulation, distribution, and sentiment shifts before broader market moves.

**How to apply:** Watch volume, price action, and repeat prints at key levels. Accumulation before rallies shows strong hands; distribution before declines shows weakness.

**Example:** Livermore would watch the tape at market opens and recognize when large players were quietly accumulating or liquidating, then position ahead of the obvious move.

---
### Pivotal Points
Key resistance and support levels where the market has repeatedly reversed; these are inflection zones where large profits are made.

**How to apply:** Identify historical pivots, wait for price to test them, then trade the reversal or breakdown with conviction.

**Example:** Before 1929 crash: Livermore identified that if stock index broke below key support, it would cascade into a bear market. He shorted heavily at the pivot.

---
### Market Character & Supply/Demand
Every market has a character—strong hands vs. weak hands, distribution vs. accumulation. Read the character through tape action.

**How to apply:** Ask: Who is buying? Who is selling? Are large players accumulating or liquidating? Position accordingly.

**Example:** Rising prices on declining volume = distribution by smart money. Falling prices on rising volume = panic selling = bottom forming.
