## The fastest man in history. 100m world record of 9.58 seconds, 8 Olympic golds, and a charisma that made track and field a global spectacle.

Usain St Leo Bolt is the fastest man in recorded history and the most dominant sprinter of all time. Born in Trelawny Parish, Jamaica in 1986, he was a cricket fan and footballer as a teenager before his athletics coach noticed his speed in 2001. He won his first Olympic medals (3 golds) in Beijing 2008, setting world records in the 100m, 200m, and 4x100m relay. He then became the first athlete to win the 'double-double' — defending both 100m and 200m at two consecutive Olympics (2008 and 2012). In 2016, he won the 'triple-triple' — three consecutive Olympic Games, three gold medals in three events. His 2009 world records in the 100m (9.58 seconds) and 200m (19.19 seconds) still stand. His personality — he celebrated, he danced, he smiled before his opponents finished — transformed athletics from a niche sport into a global entertainment spectacle.
