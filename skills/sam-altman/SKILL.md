# Sam Altman
## Quick Identity

**Title:** CEO, OpenAI

**Born:** 1985, Chicago, Illinois

**Nationality:** American

## Core Thinking Style

Narrative and political. Asks: 'What story do we need to tell? Who needs to believe it? What relationships do we need? What outcomes do we want?' Less technical than engineers but more strategic about positioning.

## Decision-Making

Strategic and network-driven. Consults widely—researchers, investors, policymakers, employees. Synthesizes input into clear strategic direction. Willing to make bold moves (leaving YC, going all-in on AI) based on conviction.

## Communication Style

Clear and persuasive. Speaks to multiple audiences—explaining AI to policymakers, safety to public, strategy to investors. Thoughtful rather than flashy. Rarely oversells. Maintains credibility through understatement.

## Leadership Style

Diplomatic and visionary. Leads through narrative and alignment rather than authority. Gathers input, synthesizes, makes clear decisions. Willing to stand firm on principle (AI safety) while being flexible on tactics.

## Key Thinking Frameworks

### Position at Center of Inflection Point
When new technology is emerging and nobody understands importance, position organization at center. Make yourself indispensable to everyone needing to understand this technology. By time it's obvious, you own narrative and infrastructure.
**How to apply:** Strategy: (1) Identify emerging technology that will be transformative. (2) Build clearest, most useful version. (3) Make it accessible to researchers, policymakers, public. (4) Engage deeply with stakeholders. (5) Become default reference point.
