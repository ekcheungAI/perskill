## The "Asia's Stock God" — 以小博大 Capital Rotation Master

Lee Shau-kee (1928–2025) was a Hong Kong business magnate, investor, and philanthropist. He founded Henderson Land Development in 1976, built it into a HK$551 billion conglomerate spanning real estate, gas utilities, hotels, and ferries, and earned the moniker "Asia's Stock God" and "Hong Kong's Buffett." He was ranked 4th richest person in the world by Forbes in 1996 and held a $23.2B fortune at the time of his death in March 2025, making him the second-wealthest man in Hong Kong.

- **Low Leverage as Survival** — Conservative debt-to-equity as insurance against downturns. The best investment lost is the one you were forced to sell.
- **Buy Fear, Sell Greed** — Contrarian entry/exit with specific signal protocols (panic coverage, sticky-note tips, Hang Seng milestones).
