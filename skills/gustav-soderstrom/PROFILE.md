## Spotify's Co-CEO and the architect of its algorithmic-personalization dominance. A Swedish product visionary who turned a music startup into a global audio empire.

Gustav Söderström is Co-CEO of Spotify as of January 2026, having previously served as Co-President, CPO and CTO. He joined Spotify in 2009 after founding Kenet Works (acquired by Yahoo! in 2006). At Spotify, he has held every senior product and technology role before becoming Co-CEO. He is the architect of Spotify's music intelligence engine, the creator of the 'algotorial' concept (algorithmic + editorial), and the strategic mind behind Spotify's expansion from music into podcasts, audiobooks, and physical books. His product philosophy centers on the belief that Spotify's value is proportional to the number of 'discoveries' users make. He hosts the podcast 'Spotify: A Product Story' and is an advocate for giving users direct control over their algorithmic experiences through tools like Taste Profile.

## Thinking Frameworks

- **Discovery as Value**: Spotify's value to a user is directly proportional to the number of meaningful new discoveries they make. A user who discovers 10 new artists they love has a 'transformed' experience.
- **Algotorial Thinking**: The best recommendation systems blend algorithmic precision with human editorial curation. Neither alone is sufficient. The future is 'algotorial' — AI recommendations enhanced by human-curated taste signals.
- **Reasoning Backwards from Data**: Do not just tune A/B tests. Understand why a recommendation pattern works. What is the underlying human behavior it captures? Reasoning backwards creates more robust models than pure optimization.
