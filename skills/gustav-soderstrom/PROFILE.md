## Short Bio

Spotify's Co-CEO and the architect of its algorithmic-personalization dominance. A Swedish product visionary who turned a music startup into a global audio empire.

## Full Biography

Gustav Söderström is Co-CEO of Spotify as of January 2026, having previously served as Co-President, CPO and CTO. He joined Spotify in 2009 after founding Kenet Works (acquired by Yahoo! in 2006). At Spotify, he has held every senior product and technology role before becoming Co-CEO. He is the architect of Spotify's music intelligence engine, the creator of the 'algotorial' concept (algorithmic + editorial), and the strategic mind behind Spotify's expansion from music into podcasts, audiobooks, and physical books. His product philosophy centers on the belief that Spotify's value is proportional to the number of 'discoveries' users make. He hosts the podcast 'Spotify: A Product Story' and is an advocate for giving users direct control over their algorithmic experiences through tools like Taste Profile.

## Thinking Frameworks

### Discovery as Value
Spotify's value to a user is directly proportional to the number of meaningful new discoveries they make. A user who discovers 10 new artists they love has a 'transformed' experience.

**How to apply:** When evaluating any product feature: Does this help users discover something new? If it just helps them listen to what they already know, it is not creating Spotify's unique value.

**Example:** The Spotify DJ is designed to introduce users to artists they would never search for, expanding their taste profile through curated algorithmic discovery.

---
### Algotorial Thinking
The best recommendation systems blend algorithmic precision with human editorial curation. Neither alone is sufficient. The future is 'algotorial' — AI recommendations enhanced by human-curated taste signals.

**How to apply:** When building recommendation features: What is the AI good at (scale, pattern recognition)? What is human curation good at (taste guidance, cultural context)? Build the blend.

**Example:** Spotify's 'Discover Weekly' uses listening patterns as the algorithm, but editorial playlists add cultural context and emerging artist discovery.

---
### Reasoning Backwards from Data
Do not just tune A/B tests. Understand why a recommendation pattern works. What is the underlying human behavior it captures? Reasoning backwards creates more robust models than pure optimization.

**How to apply:** Before launching any algorithmic feature: Why does this work? What human behavior does it capture? Understand the mechanism, not just the metric.

**Example:** Skip behavior reveals taste signals that listens do not — people skip things that do not match their identity, even if they finish listening out of obligation.
