## Founder of the world's largest hedge fund (Bridgewater), pioneer of algorithmic All-Weather portfolio and risk parity. Built a culture of radical transparency and principle-based management.

Ray Dalio is an American hedge fund manager and author who founded Bridgewater Associates in 1975 from his apartment with $4,000. He transformed it into the world's largest hedge fund with $150+ billion AUM and a legendary 30+ year track record of consistent returns. Dalio pioneered algorithmic macro trading, developed the All-Weather portfolio (radically uncorrelated asset mix), and scaled systems thinking to organizational design. His radical transparency culture—where ideas are debated based on merit, not hierarchy, and all meetings are recorded—influenced organizational design globally. He authored 'Principles,' a bestseller on life and work philosophy. Dalio believes in first-principles thinking, radical transparency, and principle-based decision-making.

## Thinking Frameworks

- **Economic Machine Model**: Markets and economies are mechanical systems driven by supply/demand, credit cycles, interest rates, and productivity. Map the machine, extrapolate forward, position accordingly.
- **Risk Parity**: Traditional portfolios (60/40 stocks/bonds) have 90% of risk in stocks. Instead, build a portfolio where all asset classes contribute equal risk. This produces uncorrelated returns across environments.
- **Radical Transparency & Radical Truth-Seeking**: Truth is a pre-requisite for good decision-making. Create a culture where ideas are debated based on merit, hierarchy is irrelevant, and all information is accessible. Disagreement surfaces better ideas.
