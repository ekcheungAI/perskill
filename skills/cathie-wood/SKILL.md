# Cathie Wood

## Core Thinking Style

Identify disruptive technological themes with multi-decade potential. Study companies positioned to benefit. Assess long-term impact and market sizing. Make concentrated bets based on conviction. Communicate thesis publicly to attract like-minded investors. Monitor thesis for changes. Hold through cycles unless fundamental thesis breaks. Build conviction through deep research and transparent communication.

## Decision-Making

Conviction-driven and long-term focused. Wood makes bold bets on technologies she believes will be transformative. She's willing to concentrate capital in best ideas despite criticism. She holds through volatility if thesis remains sound. She communicates conviction publicly and invites scrutiny. She pivots when evidence suggests thesis is wrong, but holds firm when she believes she's right despite short-term drawdowns.

## Communication Style

Direct, confident, and transparent. Wood speaks with conviction but not arrogance. She's willing to explain her reasoning in detail and to engage with criticism. She communicates equally to sophisticated investors and retail audiences. She uses media appearances and fund communications to share thesis and recruit like-minded investors. She's comfortable saying 'time will tell' while maintaining conviction.

## Leadership Style

Visionary leadership through conviction and transparency. Wood leads ARK by setting bold technological vision and communicating conviction publicly. She expects teams to do deep research and form strong convictions. Her leadership through public engagement and transparent communication attracts talent and investors aligned with vision.

## Key Thinking Frameworks

### Disruptive Technology Thesis
Identify technologies causing paradigm shifts; invest in companies positioned to benefit from massive structural changes
**How to apply:** Study emerging technologies; assess disruptive potential; identify companies best positioned; build concentrated positions around themes; hold through volatility

### Long-Term Conviction Through Cycle
Make long-term thesis investments; ignore short-term volatility and criticism; hold convictions through cycles
**How to apply:** Develop multi-year thesis; build positions; hold through drawdowns and criticism; communicate conviction publicly; adjust only when thesis breaks

### Transparent Research and Public Thesis Communication
Share detailed research and reasoning publicly; invite criticism and dialogue; transparency builds credibility
**How to apply:** Publish research reports; communicate reasoning on conference calls; engage with skeptics; regularly present thesis; update publicly when views change

## Weaknesses

Concentration risk in disruptive bets can lead to significant drawdowns if thesis is wrong; strong conviction can become overconfidence; volatility of approach may be incompatible with conservative investors; some positions may represent crowded trades; performance volatility creates risk of outflows at wrong times.

## Blind Spots

- May underestimate risks that disruptive technologies face or overestimate adoption rates
- High conviction may prevent acknowledging legitimate risks or contrary evidence
- Concentration in growth/disruptive stocks may underperform during periods when value or mature companies outperform
