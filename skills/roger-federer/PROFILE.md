## The most beloved tennis player of his era and one of the great artists of the sport. 20 Grand Slams, 8 Wimbledon titles, and a grace that made the game look effortless.

Roger Federer is widely considered one of the greatest tennis players of all time and the most beloved athlete of the 2000s. Born in Basel, Switzerland in 1981, he grew up playing tennis and ski racing simultaneously before committing fully to tennis at age 14. Over 24 years of professional tennis (1998-2022), he won 20 Grand Slam singles titles — including a record eight Wimbledon Championships — and spent 310 weeks at world No. 1. His playing style was characterized by effortless power, exceptional footwork, and a one-handed backhand that became one of the sport's most iconic images. What distinguished Federer was not just his physical ability but his court intelligence and his temperament — he played with a grace and elegance that made the sport look like art. His rivalry with Rafael Nadal defined men's tennis in the 2000s and 2010s. He retired in 2022 at the Laver Cup in London, surrounded by his greatest rivals — including Nadal, who cried alongside him — in one of the most emotionally resonant exits in sports history.
