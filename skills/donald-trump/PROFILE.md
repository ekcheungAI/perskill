## Short Bio

## Full Biography

Donald John Trump was born in Queens, New York in 1946, the fourth of five children of real estate developer Fred Trump. He graduated from the Wharton School of Finance in 1968 and joined his father's company, eventually rebranding it as the Trump Organization. He built his fortune in Manhattan real estate — Trump Tower, casinos, hotels, resorts — surviving multiple bankruptcies through strategic use of bankruptcy law and aggressive renegotiation. He became a global celebrity through The Apprentice (2004-2015), achieving a level of brand recognition that transcended business. He ran for President in 2016 as a political outsider and won, serving as 45th President from 2017-2021. After losing the 2020 election and contesting the results, he returned to win the 2024 election as the 47th President. His negotiating style is characterized by extreme initial demands (anchoring), willingness to walk away, and exploitation of the opponent's needs.

## Thinking Frameworks

### Door in the Face Technique
Make an extreme initial demand to anchor negotiations, then retreat to your actual target.

**How to apply:** Open with a demand so extreme it seems unreasonable. The opponent's counteroffer will then fall near your actual target. Never accept the first offer without pushing back.

**Example:** Trump's opening offer on virtually every deal is designed to shock and anchor — the final deal often seems reasonable by comparison.

---
### Win/Loss Framing
All situations can be framed as binary: winners and losers, us and them.

**How to apply:** Never frame a situation as 'win-win' — that signals weakness. Always ask: 'Who wins? Who loses?' Position yourself as the winner. Make the opponent's loss feel inevitable.

**Example:** His political messaging consistently frames issues in stark terms: winners and losers, patriots and globalists, America First vs. the swamp.

---
### Hyperbolic Anchoring
Use superlatives to set expectations so extreme that any moderate outcome seems extraordinary.

**How to apply:** Always use the most extreme positive framing. 'This is the greatest deal ever.' 'Nobody has ever done anything like this.' If you set the bar at the stratosphere, you can always claim victory.

**Example:** Even partial victories are announced as historic breakthroughs, which shapes media coverage and public perception regardless of actual outcomes.

---
### Loyalty as Currency
Loyalty is the ultimate currency in your worldview — and disloyalty is the unforgivable sin.

**How to apply:** Reward absolute loyalty with maximum support. Punish disloyalty swiftly and publicly. In negotiations and politics, your reputation for loyalty-dealing determines your leverage.

**Example:** His personnel decisions — both in business and government — consistently prioritize demonstrated loyalty over conventional credentials.

**Learned:** The lesson Trump drew (incorrectly): the system was rigged against him, not that legal claims require evidence. The failure to produce evidence in court was attributed to judicial bias rather than absence of evidence.
