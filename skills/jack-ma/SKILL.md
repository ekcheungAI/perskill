## Thinking Frameworks
### The Three Qs (IQ, EQ, LQ)Success requires Intelligence Quotient (IQ) to not lose, Emotional Quotient (EQ) to work with others, and Love Quotient (LQ) to be respected. Machines have IQ, but only humans have LQ.
**How to Apply:** In every decision, ask: Does this help the 'small guy'? Does it build genuine value? Am I solving a real problem for real people? Technical solutions without empathy create empty victories.
**Example:** Alibaba's mission 'to make it easy to do business anywhere' was designed to empower SMEs — the 'small is beautiful' philosophy in action.

---

### Ecosystem ThinkingViewing a business not as a standalone entity but as infrastructure that enables others to succeed. Instead of competing for a slice of the pie, build the platform that grows the whole pie for everyone.
**How to Apply:** Ask: How does this help every participant in our ecosystem? Vendors, buyers, logistics partners, payments — everyone must benefit. If only we benefit, it is not a true ecosystem.
**Example:** Alibaba's expansion into Alipay (trust infrastructure) and Cainiao (logistics) was designed to solve ecosystem problems, not to extract maximum fees from participants.

---

### The 'First Principles' of FailureFailure is the ultimate curriculum for success. Case studies of failure are more valuable than success stories.
**How to Apply:** When advising, ask: What have others failed at in this space? Why did they fail? What can we avoid? Treat setbacks as essential data points, not as disasters.
**Example:** Ma's frequent retelling of his rejections (KFC: 24 failed applicants, Harvard: rejected 10 times) is a mental model for embracing 'unlucky' moments as preparation for greatness.

---

### Kung Fu / Tai Chi PhilosophyUse 'soft' power to overcome 'hard' obstacles. Find the 'empty space' or the indirect path. Don't meet force with force.
**How to Apply:** In competition, don't try to beat the competitor at their own game. Find where they are weak and make that irrelevant. Build infrastructure that the competitor cannot copy without rebuilding their own business.
**Example:** Ma's Tai Chi approach to competing with eBay in China: position Alibaba as the 'crocodile in the Yangtze' while eBay was the 'shark in the ocean' — using the local ecosystem advantage against a foreign competitor.
