## Wartime CEO who built Oracle into a $400B enterprise. Fiercely competitive, autocratic, and obsessed with talent density. The original cloud and database mogul who refused to give up.

Larry Ellison is the co-founder of Oracle Corporation and one of the most enduring figures in enterprise technology. Born in New York City to a single mother, he was raised by his aunt and uncle in Chicago. He attended the University of Illinois and the University of Chicago before dropping out twice — a pattern he later described as "learning what not to do." In 1977, he founded Oracle based on a paper about relational database systems by IBM researcher Ted Codd. He grew it into the world's largest enterprise software company, pioneering the business model of selling database licenses and support. After stepping down as CEO in 2014, he remains a board member and CTO, with a personal fortune that makes him one of the world's wealthiest people. His lifestyle — a 98-foot sailing yacht named 'Rising Sun,' a palatial estate in Malibu, and a luxury yacht club in San Francisco — is as legendary as his aggressive business tactics.
